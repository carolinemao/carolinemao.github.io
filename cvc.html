How do we strengthen Columbia University community in the face of social challenges posed by remote learning?

— Encourage student socialization by promoting remote university events

— Offer students support through organized resources focused on social life, job opportunities, and other needs

— Support student life by organizing events such as mentorship programs and socials

Initial user research

In our first round of user research, we tested on 23 Columbia student users, who were asked to look at the website and answer questions about each page and the website overall.

Results

  • The majority of users thought events was the most important page, with resources in second
  • Users were unsure of the exact purpose of CVC, though most realized it was a kind of centralized resource for the Columbia community
  • Overall, pages were lacking in organization and a clearly stated purpose
  • People did not understand the purpose of a study spaces page

Focuses

As a result of user research, we focused on the following projects:
  • Events: a centralized page displaying virtual events, encouraging students to socialize with each other remotely
  • Resources: support students’ needs by providing info about resources concerned with jobs, social life, mental health, etc
  • Homepage: a friendly, informative page which helps the user better understand what the site has to offer
